Data structure

How TurtleSync thinks about data

The reporting form is built to capture the information that matters most to responders and researchers: where the turtle is, what condition it is in, and what might have caused the problem.

Location and time Condition and behavior Photos and evidence Human activity nearby

Below is a simplified example of the fields TurtleSync aims to capture for each report. Real data will only be shared with permitted partners and will follow local rules for sensitive locations.

Example report fields
Category Field Example
Location Beach or area name Clearwater Beach, FL
Location Coordinates 27.977° N, 82.827° W
Time Date and time June 14, 2025 - 8:42 pm
Condition Type of report Injured adult turtle
Condition Visible issues Fishing line entanglement, shallow breathing
Context Nearby hazards Heavy beach lighting, active boat traffic offshore
Media Photos or video Uploaded images for species ID and triage

This table is a prototype example. Final fields may be adjusted with input from rescue teams and biologists.

What an early summary could look like

As reports grow, TurtleSync will be able to show patterns. For example, which stretches of coastline see the most strandings, or when hatchling disorientation is most common.

Example summary - sample only
Region Reports logged Common issues Notes for outreach
Central Gulf Coast 24 Boat strikes, fishing gear Target boat ramp education and fishing line disposal.
Urban beach area 19 Hatchling disorientation Coordinate with local groups about light reduction campaigns.
Northern Atlantic 11 Cold stun events Support cold weather alerts and rescue readiness.

These numbers are placeholders that show the kind of story the data can tell. TurtleSync will use real, verified reports as the platform grows in partnership with local experts.
