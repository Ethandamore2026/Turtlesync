Press and media kit

A student built platform for sea turtle rescue and data sharing.

TurtleSync is a centralized reporting hub that connects public sightings of injured, stranded, and nesting sea turtles with the organizations that can help. It was designed and built by student founder Ethan D’Amore as the first step in a long term career in marine animal care and conservation.

At a glance

Quick facts about TurtleSync

TurtleSync is not a social media project or a class assignment. It is a live, evolving conservation tool built to make it easier for the public to help endangered sea turtles.

What TurtleSync does

Provides one starting point for the public to report injured, stranded, or nesting sea turtles, with structured data that can be shared with permitted rescue teams and researchers.

Why it matters

People often do not know which hotline or organization to call. A centralized hub helps reduce confusion, speed up response, and build a long term record of where turtles are most at risk.

Who is behind it

Developed by Ethan D’Amore, a high school student with a 4.0 GPA, veterinary assistant certification, and experience in animal care, hurricane relief, and marine conservation projects.

For organizations and media

How to talk about TurtleSync

TurtleSync is still in its early stages, but it was built with input from animal care professionals and designed to respect existing rescue systems and permits.

Suggested phrases
  • “TurtleSync is a student built conservation platform that connects public reports with local sea turtle responders.”
  • “The project focuses on organizing real world data from beachgoers, volunteers, and lifeguards.”
  • “The goal is to support, not replace, existing hotlines and permitted rescue programs.”

Founder statement

“I built TurtleSync because I kept noticing the same problem. When a sea turtle is in trouble, people want to help but they do not always know who to call. My goal is to give them a clear starting point and give rescue teams better information at the same time.”

- Ethan D’Amore, Founder of TurtleSync.org
