Behind the project

The story and roadmap behind TurtleSync

TurtleSync started as a simple question I could not stop thinking about: why is it so hard for a person on the beach to know who to call when they see a turtle in trouble? This page explains the thinking behind the project and where I plan to take it next.

Why TurtleSync exists

From school research to long term work

I have been interested in sea turtles since I was a kid. As I started learning more about strandings, cold stun events, and nesting beaches, I noticed how scattered the information was. Every region seemed to have a different hotline, a different form, and a different way to report a stranded turtle.

At the same time, I was taking veterinary assistant and anatomy courses and volunteering in animal care. I saw how important clear intake information is for any rescue. TurtleSync combines that structured, veterinary way of thinking with my long term goal of working in marine animal care and turtle rehabilitation.

Next milestones

How I plan to grow TurtleSync

This is not a one weekend project. I am treating TurtleSync as a long term platform that grows as I gain more training and connect with professional rescue teams.

  • Collect feedback from at least three sea turtle or marine rescue programs on the reporting flow.
  • Build a prototype dashboard that lets one partner filter and export reports for their region.
  • Design regional routing rules so test reports go to the correct hotline or contact.
  • Publish simple annual summaries that show where and when most reports were submitted.
  • Continue updating the education guides as I learn from permitted responders and formal coursework.

Each step is mapped to skills I am building in high school and college, from data handling to communication with rescue partners.
